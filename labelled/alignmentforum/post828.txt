Fun fact: neural networks do not obey the Bayesian information criterion. They do much better than the Bayesian information would suggest, constantly. This has bugged learning theorists for quite a while. What's the reason they do better? At the very start of the learning theory math (that derives the Bayesian information criterion) there's an assumption that the parameter-function map of the function you're fitting is invertible.
